Fascination Project Research

Experience

The personal, direct perception of something: trying, feeling the tension, and the learning that arises from acting.

Serendipity

The unexpected discovery of luck, valuable things, or new ideas while you are simply being or doing something completely different.

Surrealism

A conscious bringing together of dream-like (absurd) and unexpected elements to force new meanings and disrupt the everyday.

Proactive / Reactive

As a designer/individual/group/society/institution/community/organization, give or receive input to respond to something in order to start/adapt/adjust behavior.

Paradigm

The way of thinking that is seen as normal in a certain environment.

Background Relations

The invisible conditions and infrastructures (light, material, routines, technology, social expectations) that shape how people perceive and use something.

Metaphorical

To not say something literally, but to express a similar meaning/feeling in a hidden or subtle way.

Modularity

Different/repeating modules that can function independently but together can form complex systems and adapt/change according to the environment's needs.

Patterns

Repeated structures/forms literal or figurative as in assumptions/rules/examples/beliefs/conditions.

Anemoia

A feeling of longing for a time/place you have never personally experienced.

Supply and demand

A system that self-corrects to become as efficient as possible without our direct intervention.

Systems

A collection of components and the connections between them that together produce a function or behavior through inputs, outputs, and feedback, and are thereby self-correcting and adaptive.

Liminality

A transitional experience of moving from one environment to a new environment.

Threshold

A tipping point or trigger that changes a state into a new state.

Error

A permanent error that occurs within a system and must be resolved by something or someone outside that system.

Glitch

A brief, unexpected deviation that is temporary and disappears on its own.

Status quo

The visible rules/routines/facilities/laws/infrastructures/service and everyday practices that are seen as normal in a certain environment.

Intersubjectivity

Shared perspectives between individuals/groups/communities/organizations/society/etc that create a new reality, subjectivity that is collective so it becomes objective.

Entropy

The spread of energy within a system, reducing usable energy, until it evens out.

Decay

A process of breaking down in which materials/structures/systems lose their value over time.

Cause and effect

An event or factor that directly or indirectly changes something.
